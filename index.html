Talk - Holography and Twistor methods in AdS5

(2022 - High Energy & Twistor Theory) The formulation of a complete theory of quantum gravity has been one of the most active areas of research in theoretical physics over the last decades. In this talk – aimed at Part III students attending courses in theoretical physics – I review some of the progress made to date by building upon concrete realisations of the Holographic Principle and recent developments in Twistor Theory, using AdS5/CFT4 as an example. Here are my slides as well as some typed notes that go along with my presentation.

CERN - CMS ECAL detector

(2022 - Particle Physics) I had the chance to join the High-Energy Physics Group of ETH Zürich (CMS collaboration, CERN) to carry out a project under the supervision of Dr. Simone Pigazzini. Together with Noah Roux, I studied the properties of the early prototype of the upgraded electromagnetic calorimeter (ECAL) of the Compact Muon Solenoid (CMS) experiment with a class IV laser. In parallel, we also wrote a Python package to automate the analysis of events. This work is to be incorporated in the future CMS experiment the in the upcoming years in the frame the High-Luminosity phase of the LHC.

Large Interferometer For Exoplanets

(2021 - Astrophysics) The LIFE space mission aims at detecting and characterizing the atmospheres of hundreds of nearby extrasolar planets by means of a mid-infrared nulling interferometer concept. Under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Sascha P. Quanz and Felix Dannert, I implemented the phenomenon of imperfect nulling into the mission simulation software, and subsequently quantified and measured its impact on the detection yield.
