Particle Physics at CERN - CMS ECAL detector

I am currently writing my Bachelor Thesis within the CMS collaboration, under the supervision of Dr. Simone Pigazzini. I use a prototype of the electromagnetic calorimeter (ECAL) of the CMS experiment to better understand the challenges at stake for its upcoming upgrade in the next years for the High-Luminosity phase of LHC.

LIFE Space mission

The Large Interferometer For Exoplanets space mission aims at detecting and characterizing the atmospheres of hundreds of nearby extrasolar planets by means of a mid-infrared nulling interferometer concept. Under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Sascha P. Quanz and Felix Dannert, I implemented the phenomenon of imperfect nulling into the mission simulation software, and subsequently quantified and measured its impact on the detection yield of LIFE.
