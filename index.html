CERN - CMS ECAL detector

(2022 - Particle Physics) I am currently writing my Bachelor Thesis within the High-Energy Physics Group of ETH Zürich (CMS collaboration, CERN), under the supervision of Dr. Simone Pigazzini. I use a prototype of the electromagnetic calorimeter (ECAL) of the Compact Muon Solenoid (CMS) experiment to better understand the challenges at stake for its upcoming upgrade in the next years in the frame the High-Luminosity phase of the LHC. I also implement a numerical framework to automate the analysis of events recorded by the detector.

Large Interferometer For Exoplanets

(2021 - Astrophysics) The LIFE space mission aims at detecting and characterizing the atmospheres of hundreds of nearby extrasolar planets by means of a mid-infrared nulling interferometer concept. Under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Sascha P. Quanz and Felix Dannert, I implemented the phenomenon of imperfect nulling into the mission simulation software, and subsequently quantified and measured its impact on the detection yield.
