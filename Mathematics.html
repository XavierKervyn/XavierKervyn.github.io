Ressources in Mathematics

Unless stated otherwise, all ressources are available in English.

Transition from high-school to university

  1. Webpage with lots of ressources to prepare for the French preparatory classes (in French): Maths à Saint-Cyr
  2. MOOC by the French Ecole Polytechnique (X Paris) to prepare incoming students to mathematical concepts (in French): Préparation à l'entrée dans l'enseignement supérieur (free course, check for available sessions)

Analysis, differential calculus

  1. Hundreds of exercises, aimed at undegraduate level (in French): Analyse – Recueil d’exercices et aide-mémoire, vol. I and vol. II, Jacques Douchet.
  2. A textbook that covers the aformentioned content (in French): Calcul Différentiel et Intégral, Jacques Douchet.
  3. A great YouTube playlist by 3 Blue 1 Brown, providing a great visualisation of the concepts (for undergraduates): Essence of Calculus
  4. 6h long, but complete YouTube video of BackPenRedPen on integration techniques: Ultimate Integral Starter

Linear algebra

  1. The GOAT, Grant Sanderson, a.k.a. 3 Blue 1 Brown (YouTube): Essence of linear algebra. Wonderful animations, really helpful to build an intuition of what's going on when you change basis, solve an eigenvalue problem, etc.
  2. A YouTube playlist by Faculty of Khan that covers the mathematics needed for an introductory course on Quantum Mechanics (vector spaces, Hilbert spaces, ect.) Quantum Mechanics: Mathematical Basis

Differential equations

  1. A wonderful Youtube playlist by Michael Penn on the subject: Differential Equations
  2. Youtube Playlist by Faculty of Khan: Partial Differential Equations
  3. Still on YouTube, by Mu Prime Maths this time: Dissecting Differential Equations

Representation Theory, Group Theory

  1. Easy to read, conveys a good intuition behind the concepts: Group theory in a Nutshell for physicists, Zee
  2. A good book on the subject. Introduction to Lie Algebras and Representation Theory, James Humphreys
  3. Probably the most widely cited book on the subect, with rigorous mathematical developments: Lie Groups, Lie Algebras and Representations, Brian Hall
  4. A good Introduction: Representation Theory - a first course, Fulton & Harris
  5. A very good script on Lie Algebras (PDF): Runkel
  6. Lecture notes of D. bernard, Ecole Normale Supérieure, Paris (PDF, in French): Eléments de Théorie des Groupes et Symétries Quantiques

Tensors

  1. YouTube playlist, by Andrew Dotson (a physicist): New To Tensors? Start Here

Differential Geometry

  1. Another Youtube playlist by Michael Penn: Differential Forms
  2. YouTube playlist by the Faculty of Khan: Differential Geometry
  3. Notes on Differential Geometry for Part III (Master) students at the University of Cambridge: Prof. Ranganathan
  4. Lecture of 2008 at MIT: Differential Geometry Fall 2008
  5. Lecture on Geometry and Topology at UC Berkeley in 2015: Prof. Littlejohn
  6. Recordings of the lectures on Differential Geometry I at ETH Zürich in the Fall 2020: Diff Geom I HS20
